Dailu Guan received a Bachelor's degree in Animal Science and a Master's degree in Animal Genetics and Breeding from Southwest University. He was then trained in quantitative genetics in the Autonomous University of Barcelona under the supervision of Prof. Marcel Amills in 2020. In 2021, he joined Huaijun Zhou's group at UC Davis as a postdoctoral researcher, where he was trained in computational epigenomics. He currently holds the position of Reserch Scientist in the Department of Animal Science at the University of California, Davis.

Research Interests

I specialize in the field of computational and quantitative genomics, dedicating my research to characterizing the genetic influences on molecular and cellular traits and their contributions to economically important complex traits in farm animals. I will integrate methodologies from quantitative genetics, computational biology, and population genomics, focusing on how genetic variations affect the transcriptome and other molecular traits (e.g., regulatory elements, protein expression), with downstream implications at both cellular and organismal levels. While my ongoing projects focus on multi-omics integration in native contexts, my broader goal is to explore how multi-omic variation interacts with environmental factors, such as developmental stage. Additionally, applying comparative genomics will enhance our understanding of the evolutionary processes that shape molecular and phenotypic diversity.
